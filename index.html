Investment Interpretation

The capital-flow signal favors companies with pricing power at choke points: NVIDIA in accelerated compute, Broadcom and Marvell in custom silicon and networking, Micron in HBM and server memory, TSMC/ASML/AMAT/KLAC in capacity formation, Credo/Astera in rack connectivity, and Coherent/Lumentum in optical interconnects. The caution is equally clear: when capex expectations reset higher, valuation risk and single-customer concentration rise together.

AI Data Center Industry Chain Trend

The 2026 buildout is moving from board-level acceleration to rack-scale AI factories. The critical question is not whether demand exists; it is which layer becomes the next bottleneck.

Compute

  • NVIDIA Blackwell Ultra and Rubin roadmaps drive rack-scale upgrades.
  • AMD, Broadcom, Google TPU, AWS Trainium, and Meta ASIC programs expand custom silicon diversity.
  • SemiAnalysis flags TSMC N3 wafer supply as a key 2026 accelerator constraint.

Memory + Storage

  • TrendForce says HBM demand has crowded out conventional DRAM capacity.
  • Enterprise SSD revenue and prices are at historic highs as AI services need faster storage tiers.
  • Micron benefits from HBM and high-density server DRAM, but memory remains cyclical.

Foundry + Tools

  • SEMI projects record US$133 billion 300mm front-end equipment spending in 2026.
  • Logic, foundry, and memory capacity dominate the spend map.
  • KLA and Applied Materials are quality compounders rather than pure 10x bets.

Connectivity + Optics

  • 800G to 1.6T transitions raise bandwidth, DSP, retimer, and AEC content.
  • EE Times highlights near-packaged optical interconnects for next-gen data centers.
  • Credo, Astera, Coherent, Lumentum, Marvell, and Broadcom sit in this layer.

Power + Cooling

  • TrendForce says power architectures are becoming multi-track and liquid cooling is accelerating.
  • ServeTheHome's GB300 rack coverage shows rack-scale systems tied to CDUs and high-density facilities.
  • Power, substations, PSUs, CDUs, and thermal systems become semiconductor-adjacent bottlenecks.
